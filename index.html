The Opportunity

Strategic Land Within a Controlled Mountain Club Ecosystem

Siena Valley DST I is a Delaware Statutory Trust being formed to acquire a strategic portfolio of land assets within and adjacent to Siena Valley Club in the Vail Valley, Colorado market.

The trust is expected to acquire land that is central to the broader community's golf, events, future development, access, and water-rights strategy. At closing, the DST is expected to enter into a ground lease with Siena Valley Holdings, creating a contractual income stream during the hold period while preserving long-term value creation through appreciation and eventual disposition.

The Sponsor is currently establishing two substantially similar DST offerings in order to accommodate an initial closing followed by a subsequent closing at a later date. This structure is intended to provide flexibility in capital formation and transaction timing while preserving substantially similar investment exposure across the two offerings.

Key Risks

Investment in Siena Valley DST I involves substantial risk. Key risks include, but are not limited to:

1. Land Valuation and Disposition Risk
The value of land assets is highly dependent on market conditions, buyer appetite, and development potential, which may not be realized. There is no guarantee that the assets can be sold at or above the projected exit value, and the timing of any disposition is uncertain.
2. Future Development and Entitlement Risk
Certain assets in the portfolio may require entitlements, rezoning, or regulatory approvals for their highest and best use. No assurance can be given that such approvals will be obtained, and the failure to obtain them could materially affect projected values.
3. Dependence on Siena Valley Holdings
The projected lease income and much of the strategic value of the portfolio depends on Siena Valley Holdings performing under the ground lease and continuing to operate and develop the broader community. Any operational or financial difficulty experienced by Siena Valley Holdings could adversely impact the DST.
4. Market and Economic Conditions
The performance of this investment is subject to market and economic conditions in the Vail Valley and the broader luxury real estate market. Adverse changes in local or national real estate markets, interest rates, or economic conditions generally could negatively impact land values, lease income, and the timing and proceeds of any disposition.
5. Illiquidity
Interests in a Delaware Statutory Trust are illiquid investments. There is no established secondary market for DST interests, and investors should expect to hold their investment through the full target hold period. There can be no assurance that an early exit or liquidity event will be available.
6. Affiliated-Party and Conflict-of-Interest Considerations
The Sponsor and its affiliates participate across multiple aspects of the Siena Valley Club ecosystem, including land acquisition, development, construction, and strategic management. This integrated structure creates potential conflicts of interest between the Sponsor's various roles. Investors should carefully review affiliated-party disclosures in the offering documents.
7. Tax and 1031 Qualification Risks
This offering is intended to be structured for use in a §1031 like-kind exchange, but no assurance can be given that it will qualify for any particular investor's exchange. Tax treatment depends on individual circumstances, and investors are strongly encouraged to consult their own tax advisors before participating. Changes in tax law could also affect the offering's structure or anticipated tax benefits.
8. Concentration Risk
The portfolio is concentrated in a single geographic market (Gypsum/Glenwood Springs, Colorado) and within a single development ecosystem (Siena Valley Club). This concentration means the portfolio is not diversified across asset types, markets, or operators, and adverse developments affecting the Siena Valley Club community could have an outsized negative impact on the investment.
9. Projected Exit Value Not Achieved
The projected exit value of $48,575,000 or more is based on current underwriting assumptions and is not guaranteed. Actual sale proceeds may be materially lower due to market conditions, development outcomes, buyer demand, or other factors outside the Sponsor's control. A full discussion of risks will be contained in the Private Placement Memorandum.
