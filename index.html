The Opportunity

Strategic Land Within the Siena Valley Project

Siena Valley DST I is a Delaware statutory trust currently contemplated to be formed (the "Trust"). If formed and successfully capitalized, the Trust would seek to acquire interests in a portfolio of land assets located within or adjacent to the planned Siena Valley Project (as defined below) in Gypsum, Colorado.

The Trust would be designed to own its land assets and, at closing, to enter into a long-term ground lease with Siena Valley Holdings, LLC ("SVH") or one of its affiliates covering some or all of the land owned by the Trust. The contemplated ground lease is intended to provide the Trust with a contractual income stream during the hold period and preserve the potential for value creation through appreciation and eventual disposition. Neither the Trust nor the contemplated ground lease has been formed or executed. There can be no assurance that the Trust will be formed, that the contemplated ground lease will be entered into on the terms described, or at all.

The Sponsor anticipates establishing a second related Siena Valley DST offering for a subsequent stage of the Siena Valley Project. Each Siena Valley DST offering would be a separate transaction; participation in this offering would not give the investor any rights with respect to any other Siena Valley DST offering.

Project

The Siena Valley Project

The Siena Valley Project is the planned development of approximately 960 acres in Gypsum, Colorado (the "Siena Property"), the approximately 277-acre Winding Creek Ranch Property, the approximately 99-acre Schwan 1 Property, and additional adjacent property in Eagle County, Colorado that is currently under contract (collectively, the "Siena Valley Project"). The Siena Valley Project is being developed by SVH and its affiliates under a master plan currently intended to include single-family residential housing, a planned golf course, planned clubhouse facilities, and other amenities.

The Siena Valley Project is in active development. Many planned components, including the golf course and most residential phases, have not yet been constructed. Completion of the Siena Valley Project will require additional equity and debt financing that has not been secured, the obtaining of entitlements and permits, and the execution of contemplated transactions, in each case in amounts and on terms that have not been finalized. There can be no assurance that the Siena Valley Project will be completed as currently contemplated, on the currently anticipated timeline, or at all.

Why Siena Valley — Sponsor's Investment Thesis

Four Elements of the Investment Thesis

The Sponsor's investment thesis for the Trust rests on four elements. Each is a forward-looking expectation that depends on factors outside the Sponsor's control and may not be realized.

01

Strategic Land within the Siena Valley Project

The Sponsor believes that the parcels the Trust intends to acquire occupy strategic positions within the planned Siena Valley Project footprint.

02

Contractual Ground-Lease Income During the Hold Period

The Trust would be structured to receive ground-lease payments from SVH or one of its affiliates during the hold period. The lease has not been negotiated or executed, and SVH's ability to make lease payments would depend on SVH's financial condition and the success of the broader Siena Valley Project.

03

Land Appreciation and Assemblage Value

The Sponsor's exit assumptions rely on continued land-value appreciation in Eagle County, Colorado, and on the assemblage of related parcels. There can be no assurance that appreciation will occur or that an assembled exit will be achievable.

04

Affiliated Platform

SVH and its affiliates participate across planning, development, and operations of the Siena Valley Project. Related-party arrangements among the Trust, SVH, and SVH's affiliates will be described in the PPM.

Ground Lease Structure (Contemplated)

If formed, the Trust would enter into a long-term ground lease with SVH or one of its affiliates at closing. The contemplated lease has not been negotiated or executed. The Sponsor's preliminary underwriting contemplates lease payments that start at lower amounts in the early years and increase over the hold period. The actual lease terms (including base rent, escalations, defaults, security, and remedies) will be set forth in the PPM and the executed lease. Payment schedules will be set forth in the PPM and not on this preliminary summary.

Suitability Considerations

Investor Profile Alignment

May Be Appropriate For

  • Accredited investors (and, where applicable, qualified clients and qualified purchasers) seeking long-term, illiquid exposure to undeveloped land within a planned mountain-community development.
  • Investors evaluating DST interests as potential §1031 replacement property, in consultation with their own tax advisors.
  • Investors who can tolerate complete illiquidity for the full hold period and the possibility of loss of all or substantially all of their investment.

The Trust Is Unlikely to Be Appropriate For

  • Investors requiring current cash distributions from inception.
  • Investors who need any form of liquidity during the hold period.
  • Investors uncomfortable with development risk, single-project concentration, dependence on SVH and its affiliates, and the other risks described under "Key Risks" and in the PPM.

Final suitability standards will be set forth in the PPM. Preliminary standards reflect the investor types described above.

Key Risks

An investment in the Trust would involve substantial risk, including the possibility of loss of all or substantially all of the invested capital. The risks summarized below are illustrative only; the full set of risk factors will be set forth in the PPM. Investors should not invest unless they can afford to lose all or substantially all of their investment.

1. Trust Has Not Been Formed; Offering Has Not Been Launched
The Trust has not been formed, has no assets, has no PPM, has not entered into the contemplated ground lease, and has no committed capital. There can be no assurance that the Trust will be formed, that any of the contemplated transactions will occur, or that interests will be offered on the terms described.
2. Dependence on SVH and the Siena Valley Project
The Trust's expected lease income, the strategic value of its land, and any prospect for appreciation would depend heavily on SVH and its affiliates successfully developing and operating the Siena Valley Project. SVH faces significant execution, financing, regulatory, and market risks. Any operational or financial difficulty at SVH or any of its affiliates would adversely affect the Trust.
3. SVH Senior Financing and Capital Stack
The Siena Property is currently subject to a $42,000,000 revolving credit facility (the "Revolving Facility") with a stated maturity date of March 9, 2025. The lender has verbally indicated that it is currently forbearing from demanding repayment while the parties discuss a refinancing or replacement, but there is no assurance the lender will continue to forbear, and the lender may exercise its remedies at any time, including foreclosure on the Siena Property. SVH currently contemplates that the Revolving Facility will be replaced by three or more construction loans from the same lender; such loans have not been approved and no binding commitment exists. In addition, EB5AN Siena Fund 31, LP (the "EB5AN Equity Vehicle") is contemplated to provide up to $30.4 million in EB-5 preferred equity to SVH, and EB5AN Siena Fund 32, LP (the "EB5AN Debt Vehicle") is contemplated to provide up to $120 million in EB-5 debt financing to SVH (with up to $80 million outstanding at any one time). EB-5 preferred equity and EB-5 debt would be senior in priority to SVH's common equity. If the Revolving Facility is not refinanced or replaced or if EB-5 funding is delayed, reduced, or unavailable, SVH may not be able to fund the Siena Valley Project and the Siena Valley Project could fail, in which case the Trust's ground-lease counterparty's ability to perform would be adversely affected.
4. EB-5 Program and Counterparty Risk
The contemplated EB-5 financings depend on the EB-5 Immigrant Investor Program (the "EB-5 Program"), which is subject to legislative and regulatory changes, including potential modification, suspension, or termination. The current Presidential administration has announced its intention to seek termination of the EB-5 Program. EB-5 funds may have to be returned if individual investor petitions are denied. If EB-5 financing is delayed, reduced, or unavailable, SVH's funding for the Siena Valley Project could be impaired, the Siena Valley Project could fail, and the Trust's contemplated ground-lease counterparty's ability to perform would be adversely affected.
5. Land Valuation and Disposition Risk
Land values are highly dependent on market conditions, buyer appetite, entitlements, and development outcomes. There is no guarantee that the Trust's assets, if acquired, can be sold at or above the targeted exit value, or at any particular time.
6. Future Development and Entitlement Risk
Some or all of the parcels the Trust contemplates acquiring may require entitlements, rezoning, water-rights confirmations, or regulatory approvals to support their intended use. No assurance can be given that any such approvals will be obtained or, if obtained, will be on favorable terms or in a timely manner.
7. Related-Party Arrangements
SVH and its affiliates participate in the Siena Valley Project across multiple roles, including land acquisition, development, construction, and management. Related-party arrangements among the Trust, SVH, and SVH's affiliates will be described in the PPM.
8. Tax and §1031 Qualification Risks
The Trust would be designed with the intent that interests are eligible to serve as replacement property in §1031 like-kind exchanges, in accordance with Rev. Rul. 2004-86 and applicable Treasury Regulations. Whether any particular investor's exchange qualifies will depend on that investor's specific facts and on the Trust's actual structure as finalized. Adverse changes in tax law or in the IRS's interpretation of the DST rules could affect the Trust's structure or anticipated tax treatment. Investors are strongly encouraged to consult their own tax advisors.
9. Concentration Risk
The Trust's portfolio would be concentrated in a single geographic submarket (Gypsum, Colorado) and within the Siena Valley Project. The Trust would have no diversification across markets, asset classes, or operators.
10. Illiquidity
DST interests are illiquid. There is no established secondary market for DST interests, and investors should expect to hold their interests for the full hold period. There can be no assurance that any early liquidity event will be available.
11. Projections Are Not Guarantees
The Sponsor's projected exit value and projected IRR reflect assumptions about future events that may not be realized. Actual results may differ materially. Final assumptions, sensitivities, and methodology will be presented in the PPM.
